## 3-Minute Pitch

**The problem (30s).**
Retail crypto trading is an emotional game. Traders FOMO into tops, panic-sell
bottoms, and chase low-liquidity charts that look great until they try to exit.
The whole tooling ecosystem makes this *worse* — every bot competes to generate
*more* signals. But more signals isn't the problem. Bad decisions are.

**The insight (20s).**
The most valuable thing a trading AI can do isn't predicting the next pump. It's
stopping you from taking the trade that blows up your account. The best trade is
often **no trade** — and nobody builds for that.

**The product (60s).**
Argus is an AI Trading Guardian. Five specialized agents — Market Intelligence,
Risk Guardian, Trade Validator, Reflection, and Execution — feed a **Signal
Honesty Engine**. Before any recommendation, Argus checks hard gates: data
quality, liquidity, risk ceiling, reward-to-risk, signal conflict, and a
FOMO-chase guard. If any gate fails, the trade is rejected — and Argus is
*proud* of it. Our signature feature: **NO TRADE IS ALPHA** — when Argus stands
aside, it quantifies the capital it just protected. Every analysis ends in a
full Judge Mode verdict: thesis, bull case, bear case, why it could fail, and a
final decision — TAKE, WATCH, REJECT, or NO TRADE — in beginner or professional
language.

**The WOW (30s).**
Show it a textbook FOMO chart — up 40% on the week, every signal green. Every
other bot says BUY. Argus says **NO TRADE**: RSI 86 is exhausted, ATR means a
normal wiggle stops you out, and the risk-reward is upside-down. Then it shows
the capital that decision protected.

**The close (10s).**
Argus isn't an oracle. It's a guardian that helps traders make better decisions,
understand risk, and preserve capital. Built for Bitget, demo-ready today.

## 5-Minute Pitch

**1. The problem (45s).**
Crypto traders don't lose because they lack signals — they lose because they
take the *wrong* trades. FOMO entries near the top. Thin-liquidity tokens that
look great on the chart and gap 14% on exit. Trades taken on incomplete data.
The market is noisy, emotional, and every tool out there amplifies the noise by
shipping more alerts, more signals, more "opportunities."

**2. The reframe (30s).**
We asked a different question: what would an AI that's actually on the trader's
side do? It would protect capital first. It would refuse bad setups. It would
explain its reasoning so the trader *learns*. And it would treat "no trade" as a
legitimate, valuable answer — not a failure to find something.

**3. How Argus works (90s).**
Argus runs a five-agent guardian pipeline:
- **Market Intelligence** — multi-timeframe trend, volatility, liquidity, structure, momentum.
- **Risk Guardian** — risk scoring, drawdown, position sizing, portfolio health.
- **Trade Validator** — rejects weak setups, checks reward:risk, flags thin books and conflicting signals.
- **Reflection** — journals every decision and extracts lessons.
- **Execution** — paper trading and position tracking.

These feed four meters — Confidence, Risk, Data Quality, Trade Quality — into
the **Signal Honesty Engine**. It enforces hard gates: a data-quality floor (you
can't trade what you can't trust), a liquidity floor, a risk ceiling, a
reward-to-risk floor, a signal-conflict check, and a FOMO-chase guard. Any
single failure forces the verdict down to NO TRADE.

**4. Judge Mode & explainability (45s).**
Every analysis produces a full verdict: a trade thesis, the bull case, the bear
case, entry zone, invalidation, laddered take-profits, and three honest
questions — why does this trade exist, why could it fail, why might it be
rejected? Everything is available in Beginner language or Professional detail, so
Argus *teaches* while it guards.

**5. The WOW moment (45s).**
Our signature demo: a parabolic FOMO setup. Up 40% on the week, momentum
screaming, every short-term signal green. Most bots would scream BUY. Argus
returns NO TRADE — RSI 86 is exhausted, ATR 4.6% means a routine pullback stops
you out, and reward:risk is inverted this late. Then it shows you exactly how
much capital standing aside just protected. That's **NO TRADE IS ALPHA**.

**6. Bitget & the future (30s).**
Argus is built for Bitget — spot and futures data, risk monitoring, agent
workflows — with a clean adapter seam for live execution when a trader is ready.
Today it's paper / read-only by design and runs on simulated data with zero
setup, so it's always demo-ready.

**7. Close (15s).**
Argus is not an AI that predicts the future. It's an AI that helps traders make
better decisions, avoid bad trades, understand risk, and preserve capital. Most
bots help you enter trades. **Argus helps you survive them.**
